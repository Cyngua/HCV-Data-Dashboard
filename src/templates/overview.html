Overview

Hepatitis C is a liver disease caused by the hepatitis C virus (HCV). The disease is transmitted through blood and can cause short-term or chronic liver damage. Early diagnosis allows for timely intervention, preventing further transmission and enabling access to effective treatments, reducing the risk of chronic liver disease.
